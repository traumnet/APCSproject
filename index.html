about

This is a student project aiming to educate high school students about mental health and end stigma around various mental illnesses.

Stigma? What do you mean?


Well, mental illnesses aren't a topic people like talk about or support advocacy for. Meaning, it's not as common to receive education about mental health so much as an illness like cancer. Because it tends to be a taboo topic, there are many misconceptions and inaccurate stereotypes that get spread around without anyone challenging them. These stereotypes tend to permeate media. Movies like "Split"? Villains that are "psychos"? Common characterizations like those are typically based off of wildly exaggerated, misguided stereotypes about mental illnesses. Unfortunately for the people who have the illnesses that media typically misrepresents, that only perpetuates harmful stereotypes and ideas about them.
